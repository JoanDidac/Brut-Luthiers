Guitar model :Specs

Description >>> This gorgeous acoustic bass guitar is built from a reclaimed local old-growth redwood top, Honduras rosewood sides & back, curly maple binding/heelcap, ebony bridge/fretboard/peghead overlay, and a Honduras mahogany neck. The redwood & rosewood are the same as David’s recent redwood-rosewood parlor guitar. Its 31″ scale results in easy playability and reach, while still holding onto its dynamic punch. Like its cousin parlor guitar, this bass also features a soundport, on the player’s side upper bout. The soundport acts as a monitor, directing sound waves towards the player’s ears as well as from the front aperture.

    Old-growth Mendocino County redwood top

  • Honduras rosewood back & sides
  • Honduras mahogany neck
  • Curly maple binding and heel cap
  • Ebony fingerboard, bridge, and peghead overlay
  • 31” string length
  • 14 frets to the body
  • 1-3/4” nut width
